Compounding Process

The mechanics of the snowball effect.

The compounding process is the financial "snowball effect" where your money generates earnings, and those earnings are reinvested to generate their own earnings. This creates a cycle where the growth base expands every period, leading to an exponential curve rather than a straight line.

The Multiplier Logic

Unlike linear growth (which adds a fixed amount every period), compounding uses repeated multiplication. Each period, the total is multiplied by a growth factor.
