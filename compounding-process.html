Compounding Process

The mechanics of the snowball effect.

Snippet Summary: The compounding process works by applying a multiplier (1 + r) to a total that grows every period. Unlike linear growth, compounding builds on previous gains, leading to an exponential curve.

The Multiplier Logic

Compounding occurs when you earn a return on both your initial principal and the accumulated interest. Mathematically, this is expressed as:

$$A = P(1 + r)^n$$
P = Principal | r = Rate | n = Periods

Linear vs Exponential

Linear growth adds a fixed amount. Compounding scales the whole total. Over time, the "gap" between them represents the wealth created by time itself.
