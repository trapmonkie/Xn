Compounding Process – The Snowball Effect

Your multiplier is 1 + r. See how your money snowballs.

How Compounding Works

If you earn interest at rate r per period, your multiplier is 1 + r. For example, 5% annual growth means you multiply by 1.05 each year.

Compound Interest Formula: A = P × (1 + r)ⁿ
Where P = principal, r = rate per period, n = number of periods.
