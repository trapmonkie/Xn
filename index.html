The expression Xⁿ is the correct algebraic way to represent this, where x is the multiplier (double, triple, etc.) and n is the number of times that multiplier is applied.
Rule of Thumb
To Double, the base is 2 (2ⁿ)

Starting from $1 and doubling each step (×2), it takes 20 steps to reach $1,048,576.

To Triple, the base is 3 (3ⁿ)

Starting from $25 and tripling each step (×3), it takes 10 steps to reach $1,476,225.

To Quadruple, the base is 4 (4ⁿ)

A 4x multiplier accelerates growth significantly faster than 2x or 3x, represented by the base-4 exponential function.

Xⁿ = X is the base

In algebra, the base (X) is the number being multiplied, and the exponent (n) represents the count of steps or multiplications.
