The expression Xⁿ is the correct algebraic way to represent this, where x is the multiplier (double, triple, etc.) and n is the number of times that multiplier is applied.

Rule of thumb

To Double, the base is 2 (2ⁿ)

Starting from $1 and doubling each step (×2), it takes 20 steps to exceed $1,000,000. The value at step 20 is $1,048,576.

To Triple, the base is 3 (3ⁿ)

Starting from $25 and tripling each step (×3), it takes 10 steps to exceed $1,000,000. Final: $25 × 3^10 = $1,476,225.

To Quadruple, the base is 4 (4ⁿ)

Higher multipliers shorten the path significantly. A 4x multiplier accelerates growth much faster than 2x or 3x, represented by the base-4 exponential function.

Xⁿ = X is the base

In algebra, the base (X) is the number being multiplied, and the exponent (n) represents how many times the multiplication occurs.

Compounding vs Exponential Growth

Compounding usually refers to interest added to a principal over time, while exponential growth is the broader mathematical pattern where the rate of change is proportional to the current amount.
