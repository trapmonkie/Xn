Exponential Growth Path

A mathematical model where growth is proportional to the current size.

Key Insight: Exponential growth is determined by the base. Moving from a 2x (double) to a 4x (quadruple) multiplier doesn't just double the speed—it creates an orders-of-magnitude difference in the result over identical time steps.

The Power of the Base

The "Power of the Base" lies in the fact that even a tiny increase in \(r\) creates a disproportionate impact over time. This is because \(r\) isn't just added; it is scaled by everything that came before it.

The 1% Difference: If you invest $10,000 for 30 years:
• At 7% (Base 1.07): You end with $76,122.
• At 8% (Base 1.08): You end with $100,626.

Result: A 1% increase in the base leads to a 32% increase in the final wealth.

The "Power of the Power"

By doubling the base from 2 to 4, you are essentially doubling the exponent (\(n\)) in terms of the original base. Every step you take with a 4x multiplier is equivalent to taking two steps with a 2x multiplier, but those steps are taken simultaneously.
