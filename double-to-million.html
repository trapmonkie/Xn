The Rule of Thumb for Doubling

If you want to double a number repeatedly, you write it as 2ⁿ. Here 2 is the base (what you do each step) and n is the exponent (how many times you do it).

General formula: Start × 2ⁿ
Example with start = $100: $100 × 2¹⁴ = $1,638,400 – you exceed $1M in 14 steps.
